  Sjúrðarkvæði, first táttur >

Regin smiður is the first táttur in the story of Sjúrðarkvæði, the Faroese version of the legend of Siegfried the Dragon Slayer. This version of the story is thought to have been put into song in the 14th century, containing material from the Poetic Edda, Þiðreks saga, the Völsunga saga, among others.
The táttur follows Siegfried, known in Faroese as Sjúrður, as he is born, grows up, and goes on to slay the dragon that guards the golden hoard in Glitraheiði.
