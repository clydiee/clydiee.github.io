< Sjúrðarkvæði, third táttur  

Høgna táttur is the third and final táttur in the story of Sjúrðarkvæði, the Faroese version of the legend of Siegfried the Dragon Slayer. This version of the story is thought to have been put into song in the 14th century, containing material from the Poetic Edda, Þiðreks saga, the Völsunga saga, among others.
This part of the story delves the furthest away from the source material out of all three táttur. This táttur primarily follows Haguna, known in Faroese as Høgna, as his sister Guðrun avenges Siegfried (Sjúrður here) - who Høgna killed alongside his brother Gundahar (Gunnar). Often this táttur is split into two parts, Høgna táttur, which follows Høgna himself, and Annar Høgna táttur or Aldria táttur, which follows his son, Høgna or Aldria. Here, I have not split these táttur, but I have highlighted where the divison lies.
